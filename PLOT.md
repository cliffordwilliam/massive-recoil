# Massive Recoil — Plot Document

## Logline
A corrupt Hong Kong detective burns her career to the ground protecting a foreign stranger, and descends into something ancient and hungry beneath the city.

---

## Protagonist

**Vivian Lam** — Detective, Hong Kong Police Force. On the triad's payroll. Sharp, pragmatic, and fully aware of what she is. She doesn't lie to herself about it. White bob cut, black suit, black shorts and boots, ribbon at the neck. Looks like trouble. Is.

---

## Inciting Incident

Vivian is sent by her triad handler on a simple job — dispose of a foreign prisoner being held in a backroom somewhere in Kowloon. He's seen too much. Clean it up.

When she gets there, she finds the situation is not simple. The triads haven't been holding him as a witness. They've been using him — as a key. He has some connection, unclear at first, to a network of tunnels and chambers buried beneath Hong Kong. Ancient. Pre-colonial. Something the triads have been trying to access and control.

She could shoot him and walk away. She doesn't.

---

## The Foreigner

His name and exact origins are deliberately vague early on. He's not a spy, not a soldier. He ended up in Hong Kong following something — an obsession, a lead, a loss. Whatever drew him here, the triads found him useful and then found him inconvenient.

He's not helpless, but he's not a fighter. Vivian keeps him alive. He leads her deeper.

---

## Three Acts

### Act 1 — The Streets
Ground-level Hong Kong. Neon-lit back alleys, wet markets, cramped apartment blocks, parking garages. Vivian's turf — except now it's hunting her. The triads want the foreigner back. Her own precinct has a bounty on her head.

Enemies are triad foot soldiers and corrupt cops. Familiar, grounded, overwhelming in numbers. She knows how these people think because she was one of them.

### Act 2 — The Underground
Storm drains that open into something older. Colonial-era tunnels. Abandoned MTR stations. Crumbling shrines. The architecture gets stranger the deeper they go. Enemies start mixing triad muscle with people who've been down here too long — something is changing them.

The foreigner begins to understand what's at the bottom. So does Vivian. Neither of them turns back.

### Act 3 — The Root
Stone chambers. Ritual spaces. Things that have been here since before the city, before the colony, before anyone alive can remember. Whatever the triads were trying to harness is here, and it has no interest in being controlled.

Full supernatural. No pretense of normalcy. Vivian and the foreigner are completely alone against something that has been waiting.

---

## The Escape

The ancient tunnels connect to the harbour — old smuggling routes carved before the city existed above them. Vivian and the foreigner find a way out through water, emerging into Victoria Harbour at dawn.

Unglamorous. Desperate. Alive.

---

## Tone

Noir on the surface. Folk horror underneath. The city is beautiful and corrupt and the thing beneath it is older than either. Vivian doesn't become a hero — she just makes one decision that she can live with, and then has to survive the consequences of it.
